Learn about the four ages or Yugas that constitute the Vedic cycle: Satya Yuga, Treta Yuga, Dvapara Yuga, and Kali Yuga. Each Yuga carries its unique attributes, virtues, and challenges.

The Yuga Cycle According to Vedic scriptures, the four Yugas form a repeating cycle that lasts for thousands of years. The lengths of each Yuga are as follows:
  1. Satya Yuga: 1,728,000 years
  2. Treta Yuga: 1,296,000 years
  3. Dvapara Yuga: 864,000 years
  4. Kali Yuga: 432,000 years

Satya Yuga (The Age of Truth)

Satya Yuga, also known as Krita Yuga, is the first and most virtuous age. During this Yuga, righteousness and truth prevail, and humanity lives in harmony with the cosmos. The average human lifespan is significantly longer than in the subsequent ages.

Satya Yuga is an age of truth and virtue, where righteousness (dharma) prevails.
People live long, healthy lives, focusing on spiritual wisdom rather than materialism.
Harmony with nature, telepathic communication, and absence of evil are characteristic of this ideal era.

Treta Yuga (The Silver Age)

In Treta Yuga, virtue and righteousness begin to decline slightly. Rituals and sacrifices play an essential role in this age. Great sages and saints walk the earth, imparting wisdom and knowledge to humanity.

Treta Yuga is marked by a decline in the level of truth and virtue compared to Satya Yuga.
People still uphold righteousness (dharma) but not to the same extent as in the previous age.
Spiritual knowledge and practices remain significant, with the presence of wise sages and saints.
Rituals and sacrifices gain importance as a means to seek divine blessings.
Human life spans begin to shorten compared to the previous age, indicating a subtle shift in the cosmic cycle.

Dvapara Yuga (The Bronze Age)

Dvapara Yuga is characterized by a further decline in righteousness and an increase in materialism. The understanding of spiritual truths becomes clouded, and human actions are driven more by selfish desires.

Dwapara Yuga sees a decline in righteousness and spiritual knowledge.
Materialism and worldly desires gain prominence in this age.
Society experiences growing divisions and conflicts due to selfish interests.
Human life spans continue to shorten as the Yuga cycle progresses.

Kali Yuga (The Iron Age)

The final and darkest age is Kali Yuga, the era we are currently believed to be living in. This age is marked by moral decay, spiritual ignorance, and an increase in suffering. However, it is also an age of hope, as it is said that one can achieve spiritual growth through devotion and compassion despite the prevailing challenges.

Kali Yuga is the darkest age, marked by a significant decline in truth, virtue, and righteousness.
Materialism and selfishness dominate society, leading to moral decay and spiritual ignorance.
Conflicts, violence, and suffering become prevalent as the world moves further away from its spiritual roots.
Human life spans are at their shortest, reflecting the depths of the Yuga cycle.
